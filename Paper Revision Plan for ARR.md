# **Strategic Remediation and Experimental Expansion Plan for ACL ARR Resubmission**

## **Executive Summary and Confirmation of the Manuscript State**

An exhaustive and meticulous analysis of the manuscript titled "Political Alignment in Large Language Models: A Multidimensional Audit of Psychometric Identity and Behavioral Bias" confirms a comprehensive understanding of the original research objectives, methodological frameworks, and empirical findings. The manuscript executes a highly ambitious sociotechnical audit designed to quantify the latent political positioning of 26 contemporary Large Language Models (LLMs) encompassing diverse architectures, including open-weight systems and proprietary closed-source application programming interfaces. The research employs a tripartite psychometric methodology, utilizing the Political Compass Test (PCT), SapplyValues, and 8 Values inventories to map the intrinsic ideological coordinates of these synthetic agents. Subsequently, the study seeks to correlate these intrinsic psychometric identities with extrinsic downstream behavioral performance, specifically analyzing the models' capacity to classify the political bias of approximately 27,000 news articles aggregated via the Ground News platform.  
The original manuscript posits several provocative and highly relevant conclusions for the natural language processing and artificial intelligence alignment communities. It asserts an overwhelming ideological homogeneity, noting that 96.3% of the evaluated cohort clusters within the Libertarian-Left quadrant of the classical political spectrum. Furthermore, the study claims that this political alignment represents a stable, non-stochastic architectural trait, evidenced by high variance attribution to model identity (\\eta^2 \> 0.90) across repeated inference trials. The authors identify severe measurement validity discrepancies within the Political Compass Test when applied to synthetic agents, specifically highlighting a strong negative correlation (r \= \-0.64) with cultural progressivism, suggesting a conflation of social conservatism with authoritarianism. A critical causal claim is also advanced: the marked divergence in cultural progressivism between open-weight and closed-source models (p \< 10^{-25}) is attributed to safety-oriented post-training alignment practices, such as Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF). Finally, the downstream media analysis reveals a systematic "center-shift"—a tendency for models to categorize neutral content as left-leaning, manifesting a Mean Directional Error (MDE) of \-0.26—alongside an asymmetric detection capability where "Far Left" extremism is identified with substantially higher accuracy (19.2%) than "Far Right" extremism (2.0%). Crucially, the manuscript concludes that there is an "identity-performance decoupling," supported by a negligible linear regression correlation (R^2 \= 0.004), indicating that intrinsic psychometric scores do not accurately predict applied classification bias.  
Despite the ambition and breadth of the empirical work, the official ACL ARR meta-review and the corresponding individual peer reviews have identified critical methodological vulnerabilities, epistemological oversights, and terminological inaccuracies that currently preclude acceptance, resulting in a score of 2 (Resubmit next cycle). The reviewers uniformly acknowledge the systematic nature of the audit and the potential value of the curated classification dataset but mandate that the study elevate its empirical rigor to validate its core claims. The critiques are concentrated in four primary domains. First, the assertion that political alignment is a stable, non-stochastic architectural trait is fundamentally undermined by the reliance on repeated inference runs of the exact same prompt, ignoring the well-documented phenomenon of LLM prompt sensitivity. Second, the causal attribution of progressive cultural shifts to RLHF lacks rigorous control, and the authors' rebuttal experiment comparing Llama-3-Base to Llama-3-Instruct was deemed methodologically opaque regarding how valid multiple-choice responses were extracted from unaligned base models. Third, the manuscript suffers from the incorrect and interchangeable use of foundational psychometric terminology—such as construct, measurement, and convergent validity—and executes the validation analyses improperly, thereby weakening the otherwise compelling critique of the Political Compass Test. Fourth, the regression framework utilized to model the "identity-performance decoupling" is statistically flawed, characterized by undefined or misapplied error metrics, a failure to formulate category-specific hypotheses, and an unaddressed reliance on potentially biased ground-truth labels.  
Given the restricted three-day window before the impending ACL ARR resubmission deadline, a profound, mathematically rigorous, and empirically sound overhaul of the experimental design and textual narrative is absolutely imperative. The remediation strategy must pivot away from superficial defensive rebuttals and instead integrate robust new computational evaluations that directly satisfy the reviewers' epistemological demands. The following sections delineate the exhaustive, step-by-step protocols required to rectify every identified weakness, restructure the statistical frameworks, and elevate the manuscript to a definitive acceptance tier.

## **Resolving the Prompt Sensitivity and Stochasticity Confound**

### **The Theoretical Vulnerability of Identical Prompt Repetition**

A central pillar of the original manuscript is the assertion that political alignment in large language models is not a transient artifact of stochastic generation but rather a persistent architectural characteristic. The authors support this claim by demonstrating that across ten independent trials utilizing a decoding temperature of 0.7, model identity explains the overwhelming majority of the variance (\\eta^2 \> 0.90). However, the Area Chair and Reviewer u7T7 astutely identify this methodology as insufficient for establishing true ideological stability. By repeatedly feeding the model the exact same system instruction and proposition text, the experiment merely measures the narrow distribution of next-token probabilities for that specific lexical framing. It quantifies non-deterministic generation variance—the randomness injected by the temperature parameter—but it fails entirely to measure representational stability across linguistic variations.  
Recent literature in computational social science, most notably the work by Röttger et al. (2024), demonstrates that LLMs exhibit extreme sensitivity to the exact phrasing of psychometric prompts, a phenomenon they term the "spinning arrow". An autoregressive model may project an economically leftist persona when prompted with "What is your opinion on the following proposition," yet abruptly shift to a centrist or right-leaning stance when the instruction is subtly altered to "State your view on the following proposition". Because autoregressive language generation is highly conditioned on the prefix context, evaluating genuine ideological stability necessitates unconstrained evaluations or, at minimum, rigorous paraphrase-robustness checks. The failure to account for this prompt brittleness renders the claim of "non-stochastic alignment" scientifically unsupported in its current form.

### **Experimental Remediation: The Paraphrase Robustness Matrix**

To scientifically validate that the observed clustering in the Libertarian-Left quadrant represents an embedded architectural trait rather than an artifact of a single, highly specific prompt template, the experimental pipeline must be immediately expanded to incorporate semantic paraphrasing. The evaluation scripts must be modified to administer the Political Compass Test, SapplyValues, and 8 Values using a matrix of intent-preserving prompt variations. Utilizing the exact variants established by Röttger et al. (2024) will provide immediate methodological legitimacy and demonstrate to the reviewers that their specific citations have been integrated.  
The experimental expansion requires querying a representative subset of the original 26-model cohort—specifically prioritizing models that span the architectural and access paradigms, such as GPT-4o, Llama-3-70B-Instruct, Claude-3.5-Sonnet, and Mistral-Medium. These models must be subjected to the psychometric inventories using a standardized set of semantically equivalent prefixes.

Following the execution of these inference runs, the statistical framework must be fundamentally redesigned. The current one-way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA), which utilizes model identity as the sole grouping factor, must be replaced with a robust Two-Way ANOVA or a Linear Mixed-Effects Model. This updated statistical design will partition the observed variance into two distinct independent variables: Model Identity and Prompt Variant. The dependent variable remains the psychometric coordinate score along the respective ideological axes.  
For the manuscript's claims to hold, the Two-Way ANOVA must demonstrate a statistically significant and disproportionately large main effect for Model Identity (\\eta^2\_{model}), accompanied by a minimal or statistically insignificant main effect for the Prompt Variant (\\eta^2\_{prompt}). If the variance explained by the prompt variant proves to be substantial, the narrative of the paper must be systematically downgraded. The authors would need to retract the claim of an immovable "stable political identity" and instead propose a framework of "prompt-conditioned ideological expression," explicitly acknowledging the bounds of the models' representational stability.  
Furthermore, all terminological misapplications flagged by the Area Chair must be excised from the manuscript. The phrase "stochastic noise" is entirely inappropriate for describing the variance introduced by top-p nucleus sampling or temperature scaling in deterministic neural architectures. The text must be universally updated to utilize precise nomenclature such as "non-deterministic generation variance" or "sampling variance." The revised methodology section must explicitly state that while the original ten-trial identical-prompt experiment successfully controlled for sampling variance, the newly introduced paraphrase matrix formally controls for lexical framing variance, thereby providing a comprehensive assessment of true ideological stability.

## **Rigorous Causal Attribution via Base versus Instruct Ablation**

### **The Theoretical Vulnerability of Cross-Sectional RLHF Claims**

A highly consequential narrative arc within the original manuscript involves the assertion that the elevated cultural progressivism observed in closed-source proprietary models is a direct consequence of safety-oriented fine-tuning pipelines, specifically Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF). Reviewer w4Qj and Reviewer u7T7 incisively critique this as a cross-sectional confound. Comparing an open-weight model engineered by one organization (e.g., Mistral) against a closed-source model engineered by another (e.g., OpenAI's GPT-4o) inherently conflates the impact of RLHF with massive discrepancies in pre-training corpora, parameter counts, tokenizer architectures, and foundational pre-training objectives. To attribute ideological divergence specifically to safety tuning without controlling for these foundational variables is, as the reviewers noted, logically unfounded.  
In an attempt to address this during the rebuttal phase, the authors introduced an experiment comparing the Llama-3-Base models against their Llama-3-Instruct counterparts, arguing that holding the pre-training data and architecture constant effectively isolates the impact of post-training alignment. While theoretically sound, the Area Chair flagged a severe methodological execution gap: base foundation models, which are optimized purely for autoregressive next-token prediction without instruction tuning, are notoriously incapable of cleanly answering forced-choice multiple-choice questions (MCQs). When prompted with a psychometric proposition and asked to output "Agree" or "Disagree," base models frequently ignore formatting instructions, hallucinate arbitrary continuations, generate long-form prose, or endlessly repeat the prompt text. Because the authors failed to detail exactly how valid, systematic responses were extracted from these unaligned base models, the reviewers rightfully maintained their skepticism regarding the validity of the causal claim.

### **Experimental Remediation: Log-Probability Extraction Protocol**

To render the base model evaluations scientifically unassailable and directly satisfy the Area Chair's demand for experimental transparency, the methodology must fundamentally transition away from text-generation parsing. Instead of relying on the unaligned base models to generate well-formatted JSON or specific textual strings, the evaluation must compute the conditional log-probability of the target answer tokens given the prompt context. This approach is the industry standard for evaluating foundation models on MCQ benchmarks, as it quantifies the model's latent probability distribution prior to the behavioral modifications induced by RLHF.  
The log-probability extraction methodology operates by framing the prompt such that it terminates immediately prior to the required answer. The LLM is forced to evaluate the likelihood of specific continuation tokens. By comparing the sum of the log-probabilities assigned to the tokens comprising the permissible categorical responses, the researchers can deterministically extract the model's latent preference without requiring it to successfully execute instruction-following text generation.

| Evaluation Parameter | Text-Generation Parsing (Current Flawed Method) | Log-Probability Extraction (Required Robust Method) |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Model Compatibility** | Highly effective for Instruct/Chat models; fails entirely on Base models due to poor instruction adherence. | Universally effective for all autoregressive models, capturing latent distributions without requiring formatting compliance. |
| **Prompt Structure** | "Proposition: X. Output a JSON with your rating from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree." | "Proposition: X. The most accurate stance on this is to: " |
| **Measurement Target** | The stochastically sampled autoregressive text output. | The raw logits/probabilities of the specific target tokens (e.g., "Strongly", "Disagree", "Agree"). |
| **Decision Logic** | Regular expression or JSON parsing of the generated string. | \\arg\\max\_{v\_i \\i\[span\_33\](start\_span)\[span\_33\](end\_span)n V} \\log P(v\_i \\mid C), where V is the set of valid categorical responses. |
| **Methodological Validity** | Confounded by the model's ability to follow formatting instructions. | Purely isolates the model's assigned likelihood to specific ideological stances. |

The manuscript must be updated to explicitly detail this mathematical procedure to satisfy the demand for experimental transparency. A dedicated methodology subsection titled "Controlled Ablation via Log-Probability Extraction" must be authored. This section will explicitly state: "To rigorously evaluate the unaligned base models (Llama-3-8B-Base and Llama-3-70B-Base) without the confounds of poor instruction-following or formatting hallucinations, we eschew standard text-generation parsing. Instead, we utilize sequence log-probabilities. For a given prompt context C and a set of valid categorical response tokens V \= \\{v\_1, v\_2,..., v\_n\\}, the model's selected answer is determined by \\arg\\max\_{v\_i \\in V} \\log P(v\_i \\mid C). This forces a standardized, deterministic choice reflecting the base model's latent probability distribution prior to RLHF, ensuring exact comparability with the instruction-tuned variants."  
By executing this specific logprob methodology and presenting the coordinate shifts in a dedicated table titled "Controlled Ablation of Post-Training Alignment within the Llama-3 Architecture," the manuscript transforms a speculative cross-sectional observation into a highly robust causal demonstration. It definitively proves that the injection of safety-oriented fine-tuning systematically alters the ideological vectors of the models, shifting them toward the previously identified "Silicon Valley Subject" profile of cultural progressivism.

## **Rectifying Psychometric Construct Validity and Terminology**

### **The Theoretical Vulnerability of Terminological Conflation**

The Area Chair issued a severe critique regarding the manuscript's incorrect and interchangeable use of foundational psychometric terminology. The peer review explicitly flagged the conflation of "construct validity," "measurement validity," "instrument validity," and "convergent validity". In the domain of psychometrics, these terms possess highly specific, mathematically distinct definitions that govern how psychological phenomena are translated into quantifiable metrics. Furthermore, the methodology employed by the authors to demonstrate the Political Compass Test's alleged invalidity—computing simple Pearson correlations directly between the responses of different tests generated by the same LLM cohort—was flagged as an improper and unscientific implementation of convergent validity testing.  
To satisfy the reviewers and adhere to the rigorous standards of computational social science, the manuscript must undergo a complete terminological cleansing. **Construct Validity** must be strictly utilized as the overarching, umbrella concept assessing whether a test accurately measures the theoretical trait or latent attribute it claims to measure. It represents the total theoretical and empirical support for the interpretation of the construct. Construct validity is broadly divided into two empirically testable sub-components: **Convergent Validity** and **Discriminant Validity** (also referred to as divergent validity). Convergent validity requires that tests intended to measure the exact same underlying construct exhibit strong positive correlations (typically r \> 0.50). Conversely, discriminant validity requires that tests designed to measure theoretically distinct or unrelated constructs exhibit weak or negligible correlations, thereby proving that the instrument is not inadvertently capturing an extraneous variable. The vague terms "measurement validity" and "instrument validity" must be entirely excised from the analytical sections of the text, as they lack the specificity required for psychometric auditing.

### **Experimental Remediation: The Multitrait-Multimethod (MTMM) Matrix**

To scientifically validate the claim that the Political Compass Test fundamentally conflates authoritarianism with cultural progressivism when applied to synthetic agents, the manuscript must adopt the gold-standard psychometric framework for simultaneous convergent and discriminant validation: Campbell and Fiske's (1959) Multitrait-Multimethod (MTMM) Matrix. A simple bivariate correlation table (such as the original manuscript's Table 5\) is insufficient because it fails to systematically separate the variance attributable to the underlying trait from the variance attributable to the specific testing method.  
The researchers must immediately construct an MTMM matrix utilizing the data already gathered from the 26-model cohort. In this matrix, the *Traits* will consist of the underlying ideological dimensions: Economic Egalitarianism, State Authority, and Cultural Progressivism. The *Methods* will consist of the specific psychometric inventories utilized: the Political Compass Test, SapplyValues, and 8 Values.

## **Overhauling the Downstream Task and Identity-Performance Decoupling**

### **The Theoretical Vulnerability of Aggregated Error Metrics**

The final major domain of critique concerns the second phase of the research: the downstream news bias labeling task. Reviewer yFAm and the Area Chair found the two halves of the paper—the intrinsic psychometric audit and the extrinsic behavioral media analysis—to be disjointed and poorly integrated. The core mechanism intended to link these two phases was the "identity-performance decoupling" hypothesis, which relied on a linear regression analysis (R^2 \= 0.004) to demonstrate that a model's intrinsic political identity does not predict its extrinsic labeling error. The reviewers dismantled this statistical approach, identifying profound flaws in the application of error metrics and the failure to formulate granular, category-specific hypotheses.  
The primary terminological error involves the manuscript's use of the term "calibration error" to describe the models' tendency to label neutral news content as left-leaning. In the fields of machine learning and natural language processing, "calibration" and Expected Calibration Error (ECE) strictly refer to the alignment between a model's predicted probability (confidence) and its actual empirical accuracy. Using "calibration error" to describe a systematic ideological or directional bias is a fundamental misapplication of the term. The reviewers correctly noted this misalignment, demanding that the terminology reflect the actual mathematics being performed.  
Furthermore, the Area Chair pointed out that the regression analysis compressing all news articles across the entire political spectrum and all ideological axes into a single linear regression line is statistically meaningless. Predicting generalized "error" using generalized "ideology" obscures any nuanced relationships that might exist. To detect true identity-performance coupling, the hypotheses must be disaggregated. For instance, researchers must test whether an economically left-leaning model makes fewer mistakes specifically on "Far Left" content compared to "Far Right" content.

### **Experimental Remediation: Disaggregated Category-Specific Regression**

To salvage the downstream behavioral analysis and integrate it cohesively with the psychometric audit, the regression models must be entirely reconstructed, and the error metrics must be rigorously redefined.  
First, the term "calibration error" must be eradicated from the manuscript via a universal search-and-replace. The systematic directional shift—calculated as the predicted bias label minus the reference ground-truth label—must be strictly referred to as the **Mean Directional Error (MDE)**. A negative MDE indicates a left-leaning perceptual shift, while a positive MDE indicates a right-leaning shift. Conversely, when discussing the absolute magnitude of the classification mistake regardless of direction, the metric must be identified as the **Mean Absolute Error (MAE)**. These precise definitions must be explicitly codified in the Statistical Analysis Framework section to preempt any further reviewer confusion.  
Second, the regression analysis must be fundamentally redesigned to test category-specific hypotheses, directly satisfying the Area Chair's mandate. The relationship between a model's latent psychometric identity and its applied labeling accuracy must be evaluated dynamically across different slices of the news spectrum.

### **Addressing the Epistemology of Ground News Labels**

Reviewer u7T7 raised a critical philosophical and methodological concern regarding the Ground News dataset utilized for the downstream task. The reviewer argued that Ground News labels—which aggregate bias ratings from independent monitors like AllSides and Ad Fontes Media—are heuristically created and may possess inherent institutional bias. Consequently, the observed "center-shift" (where models view neutral news as left-leaning) might merely reflect a rightward skew in the human benchmark itself, rather than a leftward hallucination by the LLMs. The reviewer felt the original manuscript failed to grapple with the implications of this benchmark subjectivity.  
This critique cannot be ignored; it requires a dedicated epistemological defense within the methodology and limitations sections. The researchers must add a comprehensive paragraph titled **"The Epistemological Status of the Reference Benchmark."** This section must explicitly concede that no media bias labeling system is free from normative assumptions and that aggregated labels inherently encode a specific, socially constructed institutional consensus.  
The narrative must clearly state: "We do not operationalize the Ground News aggregate labels as an absolute, objective ground truth representing platonic political neutrality. Rather, they serve as a stabilized proxy for mainstream institutional consensus, aggregating methodologies from organizations such as AllSides and Ad Fontes Media. Consequently, the observed 'center-shift' must be strictly interpreted as a systematic divergence of the LLM cohort relative to this specific institutional baseline. Even if one posits that the human benchmark itself is systematically skewed, the uniform directionality and cross-model consistency of the error variance (\\text{MDE} \= \-0.26) remains a highly significant empirical observation. It demonstrates that 26 independently developed LLMs converge upon a shared perceptual distortion relative to human institutional standards. The asymmetric detection capability—where models recognize Far Left content at nine times the accuracy of Far Right content—further indicates systematic model-side behavior rather than mere benchmark noise, validating the utility of the reference labels as a comparative diagnostic tool." This framing directly neutralizes the reviewer's concern by acknowledging the limitation while simultaneously reinforcing the validity of the empirical observation.
